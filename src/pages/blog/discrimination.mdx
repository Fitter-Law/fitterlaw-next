<h3>As a business owner, it is important to understand the legal definition of discrimination. Discrimination is the denial of equal protection of the laws and the failure to treat all people alike despite differences in race, color, creed, sex, or social position. Discrimination can take many forms, including hiring practices, promotions, and pay.

For example, if a business owner only hires men for a certain position, this could be considered discrimination based on sex. Similarly, if a business owner only promotes employees of a certain race, this could be considered discrimination based on race.

Discrimination is not only illegal, but it can also have a negative impact on your business. Discrimination can lead to a lack of diversity in your workforce, which can limit creativity and innovation. It can also lead to low morale among employees who feel that they are not being treated fairly.

To avoid discrimination in your business, it is important to have clear policies and procedures in place. These policies should outline your commitment to diversity and inclusion and provide guidelines for hiring, promotions, and pay. It is also important to provide training to your employees on the importance of diversity and inclusion and how to avoid discrimination.
