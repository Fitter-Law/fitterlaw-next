			<h2>What Are the Rules Governing Limited Scope Representation?</h2>		
												<a href="">Supreme Court Rule 11</a>
					<p><a href="http://illinoiscourts.gov/SupremeCourt/Rules/Art_I/ArtI.htm#11" target="_blank" rel="noopener" data-rich-text-format-boundary="true">Supreme Court Rule 11</a>Â requires that the opposing party or counsel serve all documents on both the attorney and the party while the limited scope appearance is in effect.</p>
												<a href="">Supreme Court Rule 13</a>
					<p><a href="http://illinoiscourts.gov/SupremeCourt/Rules/Art_I/ArtI.htm#13" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" aria-label=" (opens in a new tab)" data-rich-text-format-boundary="true">Supreme Court Rule 13</a>Â allows lawyers to make limited appearances in civil court proceedings and to withdraw by oral motion or in writing after the representation is complete.Â Â The rule includes forms for both entry and withdrawal of a limited scope appearance.</p>
												<a href="">Supreme Court Rule 137</a>
					<p><a href="http://illinoiscourts.gov/SupremeCourt/Rules/Art_II/ArtII.htm#137" target="_blank" rel="noopener" data-rich-text-format-boundary="true">Supreme Court Rule 137</a>Â allows lawyers to assist self-represented litigants by preparing and reviewing pleadings, motions, and other documents without signing the pleading or filing an appearance.</p>
												<a href="">Illinois Rule of Professional Conduct 4.2</a>
					<p><a href="http://illinoiscourts.gov/SupremeCourt/Rules/Art_VIII/ArtVIII_NEW.htm#4.2" target="_blank" rel="noopener" data-rich-text-format-boundary="true">Illinois Rule of Professional Conduct 4.2</a>Â clarifies when a lawyer may communicate with a person represented by counsel on a limited basis.</p>
												<a href="">Illinois Rule of Professional Conduct 5.5</a>
					<p><a href="http://illinoiscourts.gov/SupremeCourt/Rules/Art_VIII/ArtVIII_NEW.htm#5.5" target="_blank" rel="noopener" data-rich-text-format-boundary="true">Illinois Rule of Professional Conduct 5.5</a>Â clarifies that lawyers may counsel and coach self-represented litigants without filing an appearance.</p>
