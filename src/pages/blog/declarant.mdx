<h3>As a business owner, it is important to understand legal terms that may impact your operations. One such term is declarant. The legal definition of declarant is the person who makes a statement, usually written, such as a Living Will.

Declarants are individuals who provide information or make statements in legal proceedings. They may be witnesses, parties to a contract, or individuals who have signed legal documents such as wills or trusts. In the case of a Living Will, the declarant is the person who has made decisions about their end-of-life care and treatment preferences.

Understanding the role of declarants is crucial in legal proceedings. For example, in a court case, the testimony of a declarant may be used as evidence to support a claim or defense. In the case of a Living Will, the declarant's wishes regarding end-of-life care must be followed by healthcare providers.

It is important to note that declarants must be competent and have the capacity to make decisions. This means that they must be of sound mind and able to understand the consequences of their statements or actions. If a declarant is found to lack capacity, their statements may not be admissible in court or may be disregarded in other legal proceedings.

Examples of declarants include individuals who have signed contracts, such as employment agreements or lease agreements. They may also include witnesses who provide testimony in court or individuals who have made statements in legal documents such as wills or trusts.
